## Before you begin

You need:

- A Tero account with at least one connected observability integration
- Access to Tero
- At least one open issue in the Issues workspace

If your account has no issues yet, connect [Datadog](/integrations/datadog), then return after Tero has analyzed your telemetry.

Cost and Compliance show where money and risk live in your estate. Issues show what to do about it. That is where you go next.

Notice the filters on the left. You can narrow the queue by severity, service, team, domain, check, or linked issue. Leave the default view for this tutorial.

## 3. Select an issue

Choose one open issue in the queue.

The detail pane summarizes what Tero found. Look for:

- The affected service or log event
- The issue domain and severity
- The current status, such as **Open** or **In progress**
- The policy card, if Tero recommends a policy

If the detail pane does not load, use **Retry detail**. If you are on a small screen, open the issue from the row and use the back control to return to the queue.

## 4. Read the evidence

Scroll through the issue detail.

Tero presents the evidence behind the finding. Depending on the issue, you may see representative log events, affected fields, volume, cost, related checks, or a timeline of what Tero observed.

## 5. Inspect the recommended policy

Find the policy card in the issue detail.

The policy preview describes what the policy would match and what action it would take. Common actions include dropping, sampling, redacting, rewriting, or trimming matching telemetry.

If the issue has an active policy, use the policy link to open its detail page. If the issue has a suggested policy, stay on the issue and inspect the preview.

## 6. Follow the policy state

Open the linked policy, or go to **Policies** and search for the policy by service or issue.

## What you reviewed

You started with what your estate costs, saw where compliance exposure lives, reviewed one issue from the work queue, checked the evidence, inspected the recommended policy, and found where Tero shows policy state.

That flow, from estate-level cost and risk to a reviewed issue and a measured policy, is the core of working in Tero.

## Next steps

- Use [Review an issue](/issues/review-an-issue) when you are ready to approve, ignore, or deploy a policy.
