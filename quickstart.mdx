In this tutorial, we will review one open issue, inspect the evidence behind it, and find the policy Tero recommends for it.

## Before you begin

You need:

- A Tero account with at least one connected observability integration
- Access to Tero
- At least one open issue in the Issues workspace

If your account has no issues yet, connect [Datadog](/integrations/datadog), then return after Tero has analyzed your telemetry.

You should see a work queue with open issues. Each row shows the issue status, severity, title, affected service or team, check type, and domain such as Cost or Compliance.

Notice the filters on the left. You can narrow the queue by severity, service, team, domain, check, or linked issue. Leave the default view for this tutorial.

## 2. Select an issue

Choose one open issue in the queue.

The detail pane should show a summary of what Tero found. Look for:

- The affected service or log event
- The issue domain and severity
- The current status, such as **Open** or **In progress**
- The policy card, if Tero recommends a policy

If the detail pane does not load, use **Retry detail**. If you are on a small screen, open the issue from the row and use the back control to return to the queue.

## 3. Read the evidence

Scroll through the issue detail.

Tero should show the evidence behind the finding. Depending on the issue, you may see representative log events, affected fields, volume, cost, related checks, or a timeline of what Tero observed.

Stop here and check whether the finding makes sense. Use this pass to learn how Tero connects a finding to evidence you can inspect.

## 4. Inspect the recommended policy

Find the policy card in the issue detail.

The policy preview should describe what the policy would match and what action it would take. Common actions include dropping, sampling, redacting, rewriting, or trimming matching telemetry.

If the issue already has an active policy, use the policy link to open its detail page. If the issue has a suggested policy, stay on the issue and inspect the preview.

## 5. Follow the policy state

Open the linked policy, or go to **Policies** and search for the policy by service or issue.

## What you reviewed

You reviewed one issue from the work queue, checked the evidence, inspected the recommended policy, and found where Tero shows policy state.

That is the basic review flow: issue, evidence, policy, runtime state, impact.
