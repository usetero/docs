## 1. Connect

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/how-it-works-otel-1.svg" alt="Connect to your OTel provider" />
</Frame>

Tero connects to your observability backend via API. The default setup uses read-only access and leaves your agents, code, and infrastructure unchanged.

## 2. Build context

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/how-it-works-otel-2.svg" alt="Understand your data" />
</Frame>

Tero analyzes log telemetry and builds the [Master Catalog](/master-catalog/overview). Tero groups raw logs into [log events](/master-catalog/log-events). Tero tracks what each pattern represents, when it matters, and which service produces it.

## 3. Review issues

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/how-it-works-otel-3.svg" alt="Review issues" />
</Frame>

Tero shows [issues](/issues/overview) with evidence, recommended policies, and related catalog context.

## 4. Take action

Once Tero identifies a problem, you decide what to do.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="In your code" defaultOpen>
    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/how-it-works-otel-4-in-code.svg" alt="Fix it in your code" />
    </Frame>

    Tero can open a PR for your team to review or leave the analysis for your team to implement.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="In your infrastructure">
    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/how-it-works-otel-4-in-infrastructure.svg" alt="OTel Collector with Tero policy engine" />
    </Frame>

    Deploy Tero's policy engine in your infrastructure. It's a drop-in replacement for the [OTel Collector](https://opentelemetry.io/docs/collector/): it uses your existing config and adds policy enforcement. Policies execute before data leaves your network.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
