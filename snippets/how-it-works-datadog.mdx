## 1. Connect

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/how-it-works-datadog-1.svg" alt="Connect to Datadog" />
</Frame>

Tero connects to Datadog via API using a [service account](https://docs.datadoghq.com/account_management/org_settings/service_accounts/). The default setup uses read-only access and leaves your agents, code, and infrastructure unchanged.

## 2. Build context

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/how-it-works-datadog-2.svg" alt="Understand your data" />
</Frame>

Tero analyzes your telemetry and builds the [Master Catalog](/master-catalog/overview). Raw logs group into [log events](/master-catalog/log-events). Spans group into [trace span patterns](/master-catalog/trace-spans). Tero tracks what each pattern represents, when it matters, and which service produces it. Tero connects services, metrics, failure scenarios, and dependencies into a graph.

## 4. Take action

Once Tero identifies a problem, you decide what to do.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="In Datadog" defaultOpen>
    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/how-it-works-datadog-4-in-provider.svg" alt="Configure Datadog exclusion filters" />
    </Frame>

    Tero configures [exclusion filters](https://docs.datadoghq.com/logs/log_configuration/indexes/#exclusion-filters) directly in Datadog through the provider API. Waste stops being indexed after Datadog applies the filter. You can reverse the change from Tero.

    <Note>
      Exclusion filters prevent indexing but not ingestion. To avoid ingestion fees entirely, drop data in your infrastructure.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="In your code">
