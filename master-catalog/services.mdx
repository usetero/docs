Services are the catalog objects that connect telemetry findings to ownership.

The Services screen shows which services Tero knows about, which teams own them, which issues are open, and how much log volume and cost each service contributes.

## Service list

The Services list can show:

| Field | Description |
| ----- | ----------- |
| Service | Service name and enabled state. |
| Team | Owning team when Tero has ownership data. |
| Open issues | Count of open issues for the service, including high-severity count when present. |
| Log volume | Observed log volume for the service. |
| Cost | Estimated annualized log cost. |

Use Services when you need to answer ownership questions during issue review: who owns this telemetry, how many issues affect the service, and where log volume comes from.

## Service detail

Service detail pages show the service in context. Current detail tabs include overview, issues, log events, and log volume.

Use the detail view to inspect:

- Open and related issues for the service
- Log events emitted by the service
- Log volume over time
- Service state and ownership context

## Relationship to issues and policies

Issues often point to an affected service. Policies can also show the service they affect. That connection helps platform teams route reviews to service owners instead of approving every telemetry change centrally.

When you review a policy, check the related service if you need ownership, log-event, or volume context.
