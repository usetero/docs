The Master Catalog is Tero's unified view of the observability data you connect: services, log events, metrics, and traces.

<ContextGraph />

Tero builds the catalog from connected sources, maintains it automatically, and adds semantic context. Tero tracks each piece of data's meaning alongside its format.

## What Tero understands

For each piece of telemetry, Tero builds understanding across four dimensions:

- **Structure.** Fields, types, formats. What the data looks like.
- **Semantics.** What it means. A `payment_failed` log is a business event with operational meaning.
- **Relationships.** Which service emits it. What other events correlate with it. Whether it appears in dashboards or alerts.
- **Usage.** How often it's queried. Whether teams use it. What role it plays during incidents.

This is what makes [policy](/policies) generation possible. Pattern matching can identify formats. Catalog context lets Tero evaluate value.

## How the catalog builds

Connect an [integration](/integrations) (Datadog, Splunk, your OTel collectors) and Tero discovers what's there. Tero catalogs services, identifies and groups log events, maps metrics, and forms relationships between them. You can drill into the catalog to see what Tero found and how it is connected.

The catalog updates as Tero sees new telemetry. New services, log formats, and errors get discovered and added automatically. The goal is broad coverage with little manual maintenance.

Tero enriches what it finds. It detects that a service is a Rails app and adds known failure patterns. It identifies common error signatures. It notices which events correlate during incidents.

You can refine it too. Correct a misclassification and Tero learns. Add context it could not infer. The catalog improves over time.
