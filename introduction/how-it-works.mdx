Tero has two components: the **control plane** and the **edge**. They connect to your existing tools through **integrations**.

## 1. Connect

Tero connects to your observability tools in read-only mode, securely reading your telemetry data without disrupting your existing setup.

<Frame>
  <img className="block dark:hidden" src="/images/how-it-works-1-datadog-light.svg" alt="Connect to your tools" />
  <img className="hidden dark:block" src="/images/how-it-works-1-datadog-dark.svg" alt="Connect to your tools" />
</Frame>

- **Non-intrusive**: Connect via API using service accounts. No agents, no code changes.
- **No infrastructure changes**: Your setup stays exactly as it is.
- **Start read-only**: See what Tero finds before you change anything.

## 2. Understand

The control plane analyzes your telemetry and builds the context graph. A semantic understanding of what your data means.

<Frame>
  <img className="block dark:hidden" src="/images/how-it-works-2-datadog-light.svg" alt="Understand your data" />
  <img className="hidden dark:block" src="/images/how-it-works-2-datadog-dark.svg" alt="Understand your data" />
</Frame>

- **Log events cataloged**: Billions of logs compressed into thousands of semantic events.
- **Patterns discovered**: Relationships between services, errors, and signals mapped.
- **Meaning attached**: Not just format. What each piece of data represents and when it matters.

## 3. Ask

You ask questions. Tero answers using the context graph.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/screenshot-coming-soon.png" alt="Ask questions" />
</Frame>

- **Start with waste**: "How much of my data is waste?" is the first question Tero answers.
- **Transparent analysis**: See exactly why each piece of data is classified the way it is.
- **Full control**: Approve, adjust, or reject recommendations based on your context.

## 4. Enforce

Deploy policies to eliminate waste. Choose where and how they run.
