Tero connects to your existing observability tools and works in four steps.

## 1. Connect

Tero connects to your observability tools in read-only mode, securely reading your telemetry data without disrupting your existing setup.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/how-it-works-1.svg" alt="Connect to your tools" />
</Frame>

- **Non-intrusive**: Connect via API using service accounts. No agents, no code changes.
- **No infrastructure changes**: Your setup stays exactly as it is.
- **Start read-only**: See what Tero finds before you change anything.

## 2. Build context

Tero analyzes your telemetry and builds [context](/context/overview)â€”a semantic understanding of what your data means.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/how-it-works-2.svg" alt="Understand your data" />
</Frame>

- **Log events cataloged**: Billions of logs compressed into thousands of semantic events.
- **Patterns discovered**: Relationships between services, errors, and signals mapped.
- **Meaning attached**: Not just format. What each piece of data represents and when it matters.

## 3. Ask questions

You ask [questions](/questions/waste). Tero answers using the context it's built.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/how-it-works-3.svg" alt="Ask questions" />
</Frame>

- **[How much data is waste?](/questions/waste)**: Find and eliminate low-value data.
- **[Are there data spikes?](/questions/data-spikes)**: Catch volume and cardinality explosions.
- **[Is PII leaking?](/questions/pii)**: Find and redact sensitive data.

## 4. Take action

Once Tero identifies the problem and explains why, you decide what to do. Multiple options, not mutually exclusive.
