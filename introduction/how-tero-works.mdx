## 1. Connect

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/how-it-works-generic-1.svg" alt="Connect to your observability stack" />
</Frame>

Tero connects to your observability stack via API: Datadog, Splunk, your collectors. Read-only by default. No agents, code changes, or infrastructure changes required.

## 2. Build the Master Catalog

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/how-it-works-generic-2.svg" alt="Build the Master Catalog from your data" />
</Frame>

Tero analyzes your telemetry and builds the [Master Catalog](/master-catalog). Large volumes of raw logs compress into distinct log events. Tero tracks what each one represents, which service produces it, and whether anyone queries it. Services, dependencies, and failure scenarios get connected into a graph.

This is how Tero knows what's waste and what matters.

## 3. Review policies

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/how-it-works-generic-3.svg" alt="Review and approve policies" />
</Frame>

Tero generates [policies](/policies): specific statements about what's wrong and how to fix it. A field duplicates another field. A log is a health check firing 86,400 times a day. A debug statement shipped to production.

Policies are organized by [category](/policies/categories): duplicate fields, health checks, accidental debug statements, verbose payloads, PII leakage. You review with examples from your actual data. Approve what you agree with.

## 4. Enforce

Approved policies get [enforced](/policies/enforcement). You choose where:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="In your provider" defaultOpen>
    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/how-it-works-generic-4-in-provider.svg" alt="Configure your provider" />
    </Frame>

    [Enforce policies in your provider](/policies/enforcement/provider) by configuring exclusion filters, routing rules, or transformations directly via API. This path requires no deployment and can be reversed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="In your code">
    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/how-it-works-generic-4-in-code.svg" alt="Fix it in your code" />
    </Frame>

    Enforce policies by [opening PRs](/policies/enforcement/open-prs) to fix instrumentation at the source, or [create tickets](/policies/enforcement/create-tickets) for engineers to handle on their schedule.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="In your infrastructure">
    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/how-it-works-generic-4-in-infrastructure.svg" alt="Execute at the edge" />
    </Frame>

    [Enforce policies at the edge](/policies/enforcement/edge) before data leaves your network. Edge distributions can replace collectors you already run.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

Tero monitors for regression. If a problem resurfaces, Tero creates a new policy review so the fix can be restored.
