Tero is a control plane. It sits above your existing tools and gives them shared policy, catalog, and enforcement context.

## Observability platforms

**Datadog, Splunk, New Relic, Grafana**

These are where your data lives. They store it, query it, visualize it. Tero connects via API and builds understanding on top.

Your platform stores the format of your data. Tero adds meaning: which logs matter during incidents, which metrics are not queried, and which telemetry has low value. That understanding becomes policies you can enforce.

You can swap platforms, add new ones, or run multiple providers. Tero keeps policies portable across them.

## Pipelines

**Cribl, Vector, Fluent Bit, OTel Collector**

Pipelines move data. They handle routing, buffering, and basic transforms. When teams use them for data quality at scale, the config model becomes hard to manage: thousands of rules, ordering dependencies, limited portability between tools, and performance that degrades as complexity grows.

Tero uses [policies](/policies) that are atomic and independent. Ten thousand execute as fast as ten. They are portable across runtimes. We're bringing the policy engine to pipelines directly, starting with OTel Collector and Vector, so you can enforce policies where your data already flows.

Tero generates the policies from the [Master Catalog](/master-catalog). You review and approve them. They execute in your pipeline, at the edge, or via your provider's API. Tero maintains the understanding; your stack handles enforcement.

## Cost tools

**CloudZero, Vantage**

Cost tools show what you spend through dashboards, reports, and breakdowns by team or service.

Tero shows what's wrong with your data and how to fix it. Costs drop when low-value telemetry stops reaching paid storage and indexing paths.

## AI SREs

Copilots that help debug incidents, answer questions about your systems, and automate runbooks.

They can help with incidents and operational questions. Estimating waste requires a catalog of what telemetry exists, what it means, and whether teams use it. Answering a question about a slice of data is different from maintaining that catalog.

Tero uses the [Master Catalog](/master-catalog) to answer questions against the telemetry sources you connect.
